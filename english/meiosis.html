What is

MEIOSIS?

In many ways, meiosis is very similar to mitosis. However, in meiosis, the cell has a more complex task: it must separate the homologous chromosomes, the pairs of chromosomes that are similar but not identical; and it must also separate the sister chromatids (the two halves of a duplicated chromosome), as in mitosis.

Interphase

Interphase is the first phase of the cell cycle that comes before mitosis/meiosis. Its function is to prepare the cell to go through the process of division; interphase is divided into three stages, G1, S and G2.

G1

Transcription and translation of organelles, restarting RNA production and synthesis of proteins and cytoplasmic organelles.

S

Cell preparation/DNA duplication: DNA duplicates itself to form a new strand, thus generating sister chromatids.

G2

At the end, DNA condenses/spirals (coils), synthesizing proteins again.

Prophase I

After the interphase process with DNA duplication, in prophase the nucleus disappears, the formation of the spindle (centriole) begins, and so does the condensation of the chromosomes.

Metaphase I

The homologous chromosomes are positioned in the center of the cell, in the equatorial or metaphase plate. This organization is based on chromosomes that are similar in size and shape, but they are not identical. In this proximity of homologous chromosomes, there is the possibility of an exchange of part of the DNA, this exchange is called crossing over, it doesn't always happen, but it can occur.

Anaphase I

The chromosomes are separated by the spindle, but the sister chromatids are still together.

Telophase I

The spindle breaks up, the chromosomes decondense, the karyoteca is rebuilt around each chromosome lot and the nucleus reappears. After these processes, cytokinesis takes place.

Cytokinesis

Cytokinesis is nothing more than the division of the cytoplasm of a cell in such a way as to produce two identical and functional cells, it is the total separation that generates the daughter cells.

Prophase II

Exactly the same process takes place as in prophase I.

Metaphase II

Alignment of individual chromosomes in the center of the cell.

Anaphase II

Separation of sister chromatids by the spindle. As the DNA is not duplicated when it reaches this stage, each cell is left with 23 chromosomes.

Telophase II

The same process as telophase I. The cytoplasm then divides (cytokinesis) to form two daughter cells. In telophase I, two daughter cells are formed, and each undergoes telophase II. Therefore, at the end of telophase II, 4 daughter cells are formed from one mother cell.

Crossing Over

Crossing over is a process that occurs in meiosis during sexual reproduction, when the sequence of chromosomes sent by the father and mother is exchanged. The meeting of the chromosomes occurs during the process of Anaphase I, but the exchange only takes place during the process of Meraphase I. Crossing over occurs due to the proximity between the chromosomes, the homologous chromatids come into contact, causing the exchange, which can happen in several different ways, called permutation.
